## Brand Story

Two friends from Amravati watched something that made their blood boil.

Every month, Indian athletes paid premium prices for imported protein tubs and pre-workouts — only to discover they'd been lied to. Hidden fillers. Underdosed formulas. Labels that promised 25g of protein but delivered 18g if you were lucky. The international brands charged Rs. 4,000 for what cost them Rs. 800 to make. The local Indian brands were cheaper but often made in unverified facilities with zero transparency about what actually went inside.

These two friends had watched enough. They'd seen gym bros in Amravati saving up three months' salary to afford a single imported supplement tub. They'd seen promising athletes quit because they couldn't sustain the cost. They'd seen sketchy Chinese imports with fake labels flooding the grey market.

Enough.

In 2024, they founded Wellnza Nutrition. Not in a boardroom — in Amravati, Maharashtra, with one commitment: world-class sports nutrition that Indian athletes deserve, at prices that don't make you choose between gains and groceries.

No hidden fillers. No proprietary blends that hide weak dosing. Full transparency — every ingredient, every amount, on the label. Every batch tested. Every formula backed by science, not marketing budgets.

Wellnza was built because two friends from a tier-2 Indian city refused to accept that Indian athletes deserved anything less than the best.

That's not charity. That's justice.

**Wellnza Nutrition — Built for Champions.**

## Key Messages

### Primary Message
**Wellnza delivers world-class sports nutrition with absolute transparency — because Indian athletes deserve to know exactly what they're putting in their bodies.**

### Supporting Message 1: Transparency
We're not hiding behind "proprietary blends" or marketing budgets. Every ingredient, every dose, every test result — on the label and on the website. Take 30 seconds to verify us. We dare you.

### Supporting Message 2: Value
We're not the cheapest. We're not the most expensive. We're the right price for what you actually get: a formula that does what it says, made in a facility we'd let our own families visit, at a price that doesn't require a professional athlete's salary.

### Supporting Message 3: Built for Indians
Not just "designed for Indian bodies" as a marketing line. Built for Indian athletes who train in Indian conditions, eat Indian food, and deserve products that account for how we actually live — not copied from western formulations.

### Anticipated Objections

**"Why is Indian-made as good as imported?"**
Because "imported" doesn't mean "better." It means "more expensive to ship and tariffed." Optimum Nutrition's Gold Standard is manufactured in the same facilities that supply half the global supplement industry. We've built our supply chain to match theirs — third-party tested, ISO certified, and priced fairly because we don't fund a global marketing operation.

**"I've never heard of Wellnza. How do I trust you?"**
You've never heard of us because we're 18 months old and we spend money on formulas, not celebrity endorsements. Trust is built in two ways: what we put in the tub, and what we show you about how it was made. Check our third-party test results. Read our ingredient list — all of it. If something doesn't add up, email us. We respond.

**"Local brands are cheaper. Why should I pay more?"**
Some local brands are cheaper because they use cheaper ingredients. Others are cheaper because they're outright fraudulent. We're not competing on price — we're competing on trust. If you want cheap, the grey market has you covered. If you want to know exactly what you're buying, we're the only Indian brand putting full test results next to every product page.

**"Optimum Nutrition is the industry standard. Why switch?"**
Because ON is a Rs. 4,000 tub with a Rs. 1,200 manufacturing cost and a Rs. 1,500 brand tax. Their formula hasn't changed in 15 years. We're not saying they're bad — we're saying you're paying for their marketing budget, not your gains. Our formula was designed in 2024, with 2024 dosing standards.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Charcoal** | `#0B0F0C` | Primary background — dark luxury, premium feel. All product pages, headers, hero sections. |
| **Gold** | `#E8A020` | Primary accent — the diamond color, highlights, CTAs, key data points. Used for impact, not decoration. |
| **Cream** | `#F7F3EC` | Text on dark backgrounds, secondary backgrounds for contrast sections, body copy on dark. |
| **Forest Green** | `#14532D` | Supporting accent — used sparingly for credibility markers, certifications, trust badges. |

**Usage Notes:**
- Never use gold as a background color — only as accent/text
- Dark backgrounds are default; cream text on charcoal is the primary reading experience
- Forest green appears only in certification/trust sections, never in decorative elements
- All colors must pass WCAG AA contrast when paired (gold on charcoal fails — use cream on charcoal for body, gold only for large headings or accent text)

### Typography

**Headlines:** Rajdhani (Google Fonts)
- Bold or SemiBold weight only
- Used for all H1, H2, H3 — product names, section titles, CTAs
- Size: 48px+ for hero, 32px for section heads, 24px for card titles
- Letter-spacing: 0.5px for larger sizes

**Body:** Jakarta (Google Fonts)
- Regular (400) and Medium (500) weights
- Used for all body copy, product descriptions, ingredient lists, legal text
- Size: 16px body, 14px for dense information
- Line-height: 1.6 for readability

**Never use:**
- Headlines in Jakarta
- Body text in Rajdhani
- More than 2 font weights on a single page

### Visual Motifs

**Gold Diamond ◆**
The core brand symbol. Not a logo — a mark. Appears:
- Next to product names as a bullet
- As a section accent (decorative, 2-3 diamonds in sequence)
- On packaging as a seal element
- Never animated. Never multicolored. Always gold.

**Gradient Text**
Cream-to-gold or single-color gold gradient for hero headlines only.
- Applied to: main hero H1 text
- Not applied to: subheadings, body, product names, CTAs

**Dark Backgrounds**
The brand lives on charcoal. Light background sections are used only for contrast rhythm — never as primary sections. Maximum 20% of any page in cream/white.

### Imagery Direction

**What We Use:**
- Real athletes in training — not stock photos of gym models with perfect lighting
- Gritty, high-contrast photography. Gym floors, early morning light, chalk dust
- Close-up product photography with visible texture — powder, scoops, open tubs
- Honest imagery: real facilities, real people, real training environments

**What We Avoid:**
- Stock photo gym Bros with 6-pack abs and perfect smiles
- Cartoony or illustrated athletes
- Foreign athletes in imported settings (keep it Indian context)
- Overly polished or magazine-style retouching
- Celebrities or influencer-style posing
